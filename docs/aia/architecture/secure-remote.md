# Protected remote coordination — archived research

:::caution Historical experiment
This page is retained so older links continue to resolve. It does not document
the current AIA architecture or an active service.
:::

The early AIA Chain prototype explored protected coordination between
participants operating on separate devices. The research helped distinguish
three concerns that remain important:

1. **Transport protection** — information should be protected while moving
   between admitted participants.
2. **Participant authority** — protection in transit does not remove the need
   to decide who may perform a task.
3. **Owner control** — participation must remain explicit, purpose-bounded, and
   revocable.

Detailed key handling, message sequences, infrastructure choices, limits, and
operational instructions have been removed. They belonged to a retired
prototype and would not be a reliable or safe guide to the current system.

For the current public trust model, see [Trust and privacy](../../privacy/index.md).
For historical context, see the [AIA Chain research archive](../index.md).
